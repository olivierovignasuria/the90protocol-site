Privacy Policy

The 90 Protocol. Last updated 17 June 2026.

This policy explains what data The 90 Protocol collects, why, and the choices you have. If you have a question, email the90protocol@gmail.com.

Who we are

The 90 Protocol is a self-guided digital product (a workbook, audio, and conversation scripts) for founders in the first 90 days after closing a company. Contact: the90protocol@gmail.com.

What we collect

  • Email address, when you submit the form on the site or make a purchase.
  • Your tool inputs (for example cash and monthly burn in the Cash Cockpit). These are calculated in your browser. We only store what you explicitly submit through a form.
  • What you write in the optional "hardest part" field, when you choose to share it.
  • Purchase details (product, amount). Card data is handled by our payment processor; we never see or store your full card number.
  • Usage and device data through standard analytics and advertising tools (see below).

How we use it

  • To deliver the product and the download link.
  • To send the related email sequence and to reply to you.
  • To measure and improve our advertising and the product.

Who processes your data (sub-processors)

  • Stripe — payment processing.
  • Resend — sending email.
  • Netlify — website hosting and form capture.
  • Meta (Facebook and Instagram) — the Meta Pixel and Conversions API, used to measure and optimize advertising. Meta receives event data, including a hashed version of your email for matching.

Each of these providers acts under its own privacy terms.

Cookies and tracking

We use the Meta Pixel to understand which ads lead to visits and purchases. You can control ad tracking through your browser and device settings and through your Meta account settings.

Your rights

If you are in the EU or the UK, you can ask us to access, correct, or delete your data, or to stop using it for marketing. Email the90protocol@gmail.com and we will act on your request.

Retention and transfers

We keep your data only as long as needed to deliver the product and meet legal obligations, then delete it. Some providers above process data outside the EU under standard safeguards.

Changes

If this policy changes, we will update the date above.
